Small World is a new series of films now showing on Sunday mornings on BBC2 about children in many parts of the world facing up to important changes in their lives.

Already it has featured a film from the United States following an eleven year old Apache girl through one of her tribe's ancient and sacred rituals, called "The Sunrise Dance". There was also a film tying in with British fashion week following Kira Langer, who at the age of 15 became one of Britain's top young fashion models, and it looked at how she coped with the pressures of life on the catwalk and the demands of school.

If you missed those, don't worry as there are other fascinating films still lined up.

"Somehow Free" meets 12 year old Najibulha Ebrat who moved to Germany with his family from trouble torn Afghanistan. Najibulha had polio, and the film follows him on his first adventure holiday, where he tries out lots of new exciting activites. This is scheduled for 6th October.

Eleven year old Nosizwe Baqwa who was born and brought up in Norway and is black, recently also moved to a new home thousands of miles away - to Cape Town, South Africa. "I left my heart" finds out how she comes to terms with homesickness and how she settles down to a very different way of life. This is being shown on 13th October.

On 20th October there is a film from Finland about relationships! The Finns have carried out research on the dating behaviour of 9 to 12 year olds and it shows crushes, discos and the first steady boy and girl friends play a surprisingly big part in their lives. That led to the idea of the film "A lot of liking". It takes a sensitive look at romance from both boys' and girls' points of view. The programme's title was in fact suggested by eleven year old Niina who said, "To me 'first love' sounds stupid. I think we don't 'love', we 'like'."

"Small World" was made through the European Broadcasting Union - the same people who brought you "The Animals of Farthing Wood" and the Eurovision Song Contest. The EBU has a special Working Party for Children and Young People which gets together each year to make documentaries for children.
