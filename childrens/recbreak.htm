Yes, Record Breakers is back for more fab fun with its 25th series - that's 25 years, a record breaker in itself, as featured in the new `Guinness Book of Records'.

But just to confuse you, its back on a new day this time... Mondays at 4.35 pm, from the 23rd September, so don't miss it!

This year we've got some mind-blowing record attempts for you to feast your eyes on and they're not all from the U.K. - Record Breakers has been everywhere to track down the best action for you.......Australia, the USA, Europe and even the Caribbean!!

The regular presenting team, Kriss Akabusi and Cheryl Baker are joined by Ron Reagan Junior - and if that name sounds familiar to you, its because he's the son of the ex-president of the United States! Ron will have you splitting your sides with some of the things he gets up to in America. Ron had great fun filming through the summer and keeps us in touch with 'Record Breaking' events over there. he told us: "Presenting American record attempts for Record Breakers, I've discovered that my fellow citizens are nearly as crazy as you British - thank goodness for that!"

The first show is bursting at the seams with record breaking action. For instance, Ron meets Banana George, who's the oldest barefoot water-skier in the world, at the grand old age of 81! You'll also see an aerial stunt man walking on the wing of a Boeing Stearman bi-plane at an incredible and terrifying 4000 feet! You won't believe it...it's completely mad! We also get cuddly with 12 Teddy Bears when we send them off on a mission....their mission, which they have chosen to accept, is to try and break the world record by hitch-hiking more than 50,000 miles around the world. Is it mission impossible? You'll have to tune in to find out.

So be there with us to celebrate 25 years as television's top record-breaking show!

Top facts about our presenters:

Kriss Akabusi

Kriss is famous for his achievements in Athletics where his greatest individual triumph was his Gold Medal in the 1990 European Championships when he beat a record which had been unbroken for 22 years. Hs International career reached a peak in 1983 when he helped Britain take the Gold and beat the Americans in the World Championships 4x400 metre relay in Tokyo.

He broke into television presenting when he stole the show on the Big Breakfast in 1993 and he hasn't stopped working since!




Cheryl Baker

Cheryl's popularity grew after she shot to fame with Eurovision Song Contest winners 'Buck's Fizz'. They went on to have over 20 top selling singles, including 3 number ones.

In 1993 Cheryl left the band to concentrate on her solo career and since then she has made a name for herself as a top television presenter and is now back for her 10th series of Record Breakers

Cheryl is also a fantastic cook and as a result has presented many cookery programmes. She still finds time to pursue her love of cooking but likes nothing better than to be at home with her husband, Steve, and twin girls Kyla and Natalie




Ron Reagan Jnr.

Ron is a native Californian who trained to become a ballet dancer, joining Joffrey II Dancers in 1980 and toured with the Joffrey Ballet Company.

While touring, he pursued an interest social commentary, writing for many major American magazines. His satirical syle caught the attention of ABBC entertainment who signed him as an on-air reporter. He interviewed many diverse public figures on 'Good Morning America', before hosting his own chat show.
