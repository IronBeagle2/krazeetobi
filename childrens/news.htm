BANK HOLIDAY MONDAY, MAY 26

Nick Hancock introduces the fourth annual Red Nose Awards, in conjunction with Live & Kicking and Comic Relief.

Nick is joined by special guests:
Jonathan Ross
Angus Deayton
Dale Winton
David Seaman
Jonathan Kerrigan (Sam Colloby in Casualty)
Lisa Riley (Mandy Dingle in Emmerdale)
and Tracey Shaw and Angela Gritten (Maxine and Fiona from Coronation Street)

The team will introduce the nominations and present the awards.

Over the past few weeks, children up and down the country have been voting for their favourite showbiz personalities of 1997. Categories include Top Newcomer; Top TV Programme; two new awards - Top Pop Act and Top Pop Video; as well as the infamous "You've Blown It" Award, won last year by Upside Down.

5.10 - 5.35pm on BBC-1

TUESDAY, MAY 27

In Activ8 today, Olympic Medallist Chris Boardman is theMotiv8 guest and gives tips on cycling.

Evonne McNaughton and Gerard Quinn bring a report on lacrosse, and Charlene Clarke takes a look at lifesaving. Rachel Howells grabs a rope and reports in skipping.

5.10 - 5.35pm on BBC-1

WEDNESDAY, MAY 28

Blue Peter announces the results of its CD-Rom competition today. Viewers were asked to design a seasonal screensaver for the Blue Peter CD-Rom available later this year. The winners of the best four designs - representing spring, summer, autumn and winter - will each win a CD-Rom package.

5.10 - 5.35pm on BBC-1

THURSDAY, MAY 22

Kumiko and Gerard's suspicions about Selby and Glovic deepen and they investigate the pair further in Return To Jupiter.

When Selby and Glovic realise the children have discovered their secrets, they set out after them and a dramatic dune-buggy chase ensues.

4.35 - 5.00pm on BBC-1


North & South nearly panic in the face of an aged audience in No Sweat today, but they go down a storm after Bev suggests they sing "Ging Gang Goolie".

The band mistake a plumber for the important A&R man, Jonny Good, that they are expecting from a record company. Meanwhile, the real Jonny is apprehended by Janis, Carly, some OAP's and Romey - with disastrous consequenses.

5.10 - 5.35pm on BBC-1

FRIDAY, MAY 23

Romana D'Annunzio tries her hand at aeroplane wing-walking, known as Barn Storming, in Blue Peter today. She joins the Barn Stormer Flying Circus at a display at Stoughton Airfield in Leicester where she flies strapped on to the top of a Tiger Moth.

5.10 - 5.35pm on BBC-1

SATURDAY, MAY 24

Todd issues Liz with an ultimatum and is devastated by the consequences in Sweet Valley High. Trying to mend his broken heart, Todd starts hanging around with Reggie and begins to imitate his punkish ways. Meanwhile Jessica causes disaster for Liz and her science project.

10.15 - 10.35am on BBC-1


Mr Bronson is given a cold reception by the pupils when he arrives at Grange Hill and he decides not to go on the school trip to the Isle of Wight after all.

Tegs has disappeared and Justine is worried about him; Georgina discovers a love note in one of her textbooks and,, at the end of the day, a shadowy figure is locked in the school.

SUNDAY, MAY 25

Sarah Vandenburg and Grant Stott welcome Jayne Middlemiss to the Fully Booked hotel today.

Their other guests are wildlife photographer Nick Gordon and Kirsty Wright from Home and Away.

Grant gets behind the wheel of a rally car at Silverstone Race Track and there's cartoon fun with Dennis The Menace, Pingu and The Mask.

9.30 - 12.00mid-day on BBC-2
