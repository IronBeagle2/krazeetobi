Grange Hill

Strict religious beliefs, bullying, Anglo-French relationships, and coping with ME are on Grange Hill's new syllabus when the twice-weekly BBC-1 school drama returns from Friday, 6th September.

The school opens its gates to a new caretaker, Russell Joseph, and a new Head of the French department, Madame Sylvie Lefevre, a chic but formidable woman who won't stand any nonsense. Her daughter, Cecile, does not go to Grange Hill, but still gets involved in the school production and in a secret relationship with six-form heartthrob Joe.

There are two new pupils in Year 8, bright sparky Laurie Watson, who left her last school because she was a "mouthy little madam" and Chris Longworth, who is something of a mystery to his new form and a source of intrigue and imaginative speculation for Anna Wright.

One of Madame Lefevre's first duties is to organise a French exchange with students from Year 8. At the specially arranged disco the pupils seem very keen to practice their French, but their reasons appear to be romantic rather than educational.

Mr. Parrott is introduced to the world of poker, by one of the French teachers, and his bank account suffers the consequences. He also gives Robyn extra tuition for her maths. Rumours start amongst pupils that there is more going on.

The end of the year school musical runs into teething problems and Gabriel, who is directing, has to cope with squabbles and budget difficulties.

Poppy is continually bullied by Wayne and when she finally decides to seek help, through the school's anti-bullying scheme (run by the students themselves) she discovers that Wayne suffers similar torrents of abuse at home from his brother.

Producer Christine Secombe says: "Much of the series continued success is due to the realistic storylines on issues children throughout the country can easily relate to"
