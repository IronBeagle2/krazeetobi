THE LOWDOWN - the only children's programme of its kind on ANY channel in Britain - will be back for its tenth series of real life stories about children in the spring.

Watch out for "Strictly Wimbledon" - a unique behind-the-scenes documentary about the ballboys and ballgirls at the annual Wimbledon Lawn Tennis Championships. It will show how they are chosen and how they train, following them from early February right through to the tournament itself.

Also there will be a film about a remarkable young Romanian born artist, Alexandra Nechita, who now lives in California. She is creating a big stir with her paintings, which are selling for as much as $100,000 each. Alexandra is just ten years old and she has even been compared to Picasso.

Of special interest to Net-users will be a film taking a close-up look at how children are using the World Wide Web.

There will be more information about The Lowdown later in the year.
