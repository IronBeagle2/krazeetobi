July 17th, 1998
A day that woke me up all at once
From right, Kumagai, Ricky Wellman, Tokoi, Will Lee

Will is young.
I can't see anything. (Tokoi)
Will loves Sonic, and when I told him that we were here on this project, he was so surprised.
He even said, "When you're done, send me the DC and the software for sure!" and gave him my business card. (Kumatani)


From right to left: Mr. Kosugi, Ms. Aya, Mr. Roy, Mr. Tokoi

I'm kind of pretentious, I know.
Roy is in a state of absentmindedness for some reason? (Tokoi)
Why does it sound different when I hear it in New York?
It's like it's boiling over. (Kumatani)


Mr. Will Lee

Seems easy to play with a light touch, doesn't it?
But you know, he is playing something very difficult. (Tokoi)
The bass condition is really as expected! The bass is really like that.
He sang and danced and was a gourmand. (Kumatani)


Mr. Ricky Wellman

Oooh, what a great shot.
Tattoos are also a must! (Tokoi)
It's rare for someone to say to me, "Take a picture of me!" (Kumatani)


Mr. Ricky Wellman

A diagram of a drum set up.
I never expected to have my headphones wrapped around my head with duct tape after this (laughs).
In order to get a good sound, Mr. Kosugi became a demon.
Hair... (Tokoi)
Funky and very danceable. I was so glad I came. (Kumatani)
