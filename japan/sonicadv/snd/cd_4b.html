01. Mt.Red: a Symbol Of Thrill
...for Red Mountain
Music & Arrangement: K.Tokoi
This song was the first song that Tokoi wrote after joining the project.
We used this song during the production presentation and it is quite impressive.
"Woof!" sounds like a dog's bark, but what is it really saying?
I like the guitar phrase with the slide that appears in the middle. (Senoue)
Oh yeah! "Woof!" That's what it is!
I remember the look on Tokoi's face when someone said, "Is that a dog whimpering?" (Kumatani)
This was actually an expression of "the cry of the natives" who would be on the island.
The original is a call from an African tribe saying "Hey!"
"Woof!" I was so excited that my nose started to run. (Tokoi)

02. Red Hot Skull
...for Red Mountain
Music & Arrangement: J.Senoue
Can you tell that I'm using a minor melody from the 01. song?
Since it is a volcanic prison with skulls, I decided to use this kind of tune.
I think I made it very "lo-fi" for me. This is also my favorite!
I was playing around with various effects and such, and it ended up being such a long song. (Senoue)
This analog tape cutting technique is difficult to perform because it is time-consuming.
This song, which can be interpreted as a remix, easily clears the difficult composition problem. (Tokoi)
But I didn't use cutting.
Like turning mixers on and off, manually turning effects feeds up and down... Various things. (Senoue)
I like this languid feeling.
Sometimes I make my own so-called "game sounds" like "I'm going to do it!", and it makes me feel demotivated....
This song seems uninspiring. But that's the good part! That's what I think. (Kumatani)
I have found a sample of this song again. Yes. (Senoue)

...for Final Egg

The odd time signature of the intro, the wah melody, the interwoven bass solo...
